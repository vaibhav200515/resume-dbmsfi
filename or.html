üìå Introduction

The OR operator in SQL is used to combine multiple conditions in a WHERE clause.

A row is returned if any one of the specified conditions is true.

This operator is helpful when you want to retrieve rows that match at least one of several conditions.

üìò Syntax

SELECT column1, column2, ...
FROM table_name
WHERE condition1 OR condition2 OR ...;

üßæ Example: Select students who are either younger than 18 or enrolled in BBA

WHERE age < 18 OR course = 'BBA';
