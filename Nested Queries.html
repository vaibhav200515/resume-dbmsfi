SQL Nested Queries

üìå Introduction

Nested queries, also known as subqueries, are SQL queries embedded inside another query.

They are typically used to perform operations that require multiple steps, such as filtering data based on results from another query.

Nested queries can appear in the WHERE, FROM, or SELECT clauses and are enclosed within parentheses.

üìò Syntax

SELECT column_name
FROM table_name
WHERE column_name OPERATOR (SELECT column_name FROM table_name WHERE condition);

üßæ Example: Find employees who earn more than the average salary

SELECT name, salary 
FROM employees 
WHERE salary > (SELECT AVG(salary) FROM employees);

Clause Description

Subquery: SELECT AVG(salary) FROM employeesCalculates average salary of all employees
