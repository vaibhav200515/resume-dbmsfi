SQL Operators

üìå Introduction

SQL operators are symbols or keywords used to perform operations on data, such as comparisons, logical decisions, or calculations. They are often used in WHERE clauses or in expressions with SELECT, UPDATE, and DELETE statements.

üî¢ Arithmetic Operators

Used to perform mathematical calculations on numeric data.

OperatorDescriptionExampleResult
+AdditionSELECT 10 + 5 AS Result;15
-SubtractionSELECT 20 - 8 AS Result;12
*MultiplicationSELECT 6 * 7 AS Result;42
/DivisionSELECT 25 / 5 AS Result;5
%ModulusSELECT 10 % 3 AS Result;1

üîç Comparison Operators

Used to compare two values in conditions like WHERE clauses.

OperatorDescriptionExampleUse Case
=EqualWHERE age = 20Find students aged 20
!= or <>Not EqualWHERE course != 'BCA'Find students not in BCA
>Greater ThanWHERE marks > 70Find marks above 70
<Less ThanWHERE age < 18Find minors
>=Greater Than or EqualWHERE salary >= 50000Find high salary employees
<=Less Than or EqualWHERE age <= 25Find youth

üîó Logical Operators

Used to combine multiple conditions in SQL statements.

OperatorDescriptionExampleUse Case
ANDAll conditions must be trueWHERE age > 18 AND course = 'BCA'BCA students above 18
ORAt least one condition is trueWHERE city = 'Delhi' OR city = 'Mumbai'Students from Delhi or Mumbai
NOTNegates a conditionWHERE NOT (course = 'B.Tech')Students not in B.Tech

üìò Real Example

SELECT * FROM students
WHERE course = 'BCA' AND age >= 20;

üîé This will return all students enrolled in BCA and are at least 20 years old.
